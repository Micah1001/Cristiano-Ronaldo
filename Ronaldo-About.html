What Was His Club Career Like?

Early Life

Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro, who was born on February 5, 1985, is from Funchal, Portugal, specifically the island of Madeira, which is located off the southwestern coast of the country. He is the youngest of four children, his parents being Maria Dolores dos Santos and José Dinis Aveiro. He was named after Ronald Reagan, the former U.S. President, because his father was a big fan of Reagan's acting. Ronaldo's childhood was spent in a mostly working-class neighborhood, where he lived in a simple house with a tin roof that overlooked the ocean. The family was so impoverished that his mother, Dolores Aveiro, even contemplated ending her pregnancy and attempted a home remedy to do so. His father served in the Portuguese Army and fought two wars in Angola and Mozambique, returning home to unemployment and difficult times. He was introduced to the sport of soccer by his father, who at one time held a job as an equipment manager for a local youth soccer club. His early life was characterized by financial hardship, which was made worse by his father's heavy drinking. In order to support the family and try to create some level of financial stability, Ronaldo's mother worked as both a cook and a cleaner. Young Ronaldo often encouraged his father to get help for his alcohol addiction, but his father refused to accept any such help.

By the age of 10 years old, Ronaldo had already gained recognition as a remarkably talented player, completely immersed in the world of soccer. His godfather, Fernao Sousa, recounted to British journalists that as a boy, all Ronaldo wanted was to play football. Sousa added that Ronaldo loved the game so much that he would skip meals or even climb out of his bedroom window with a ball when he was supposed to be doing his schoolwork. As Ronaldo entered his teenage years, his talent and reputation continued to grow significantly. Cristiano had trouble controlling his temper and was expelled from school for throwing a chair at a teacher. He assumed he’d become a village fisherman and wasn’t interested in academics. Cristiano was obsessed with football, however, and quit school at age 14 to practice full time. At age 15 Ronaldo was diagnosed with a heart condition that could have forced him to give up his playing career that necessitated surgery, but he was sidelined only briefly and made a full recovery, resuming training a few days later. After a period with Nacional da liha da Madeira where he starred, It wasn't long before he started to catch the attention of other big Portuguese clubs. Among Sporting was Benfica, a team Cristiano and his father followed as a young boy. However he eventually chose to play for Sporting which was a team his mother loved and followed as she was growing up, to play with the likes of Figo ,a legend of the game, was the dream for her son.

1st Big Move

At the age of sixteen, Ronaldo caught the attention of former Liverpool manager Gérard Houllier. However, Liverpool had no plans to sign him at the time, believing he was still too young and needed more time to refine his skills. Despite this, Ronaldo continued to impress, particularly with a dazzling performance for Portugal against Manchester United. His remarkable footwork and exceptional talent left even his opponents in awe. He made such a strong impression that several Manchester United players urged their manager to pursue signing him, a request the club soon acted upon. In 2001, Manchester United secured Ronaldo’s transfer for a fee exceeding £12 million, an amount surpassing $14 million in U.S. dollars. This made him the most expensive player ever signed at his age. However, it was during the summer of 2003, when Sporting faced off against Manchester United and emerged victorious, that Ronaldo truly caught the eye of Manchester United’s legendary manager, Sir Alex Ferguson. Upon joining the club, Ronaldo became the first Portuguese player in Manchester United’s history. Initially, he asked to wear the number 28 jersey, as he felt unsure about living up to the legacy of past icons like George Best and David Beckham, who had famously worn the number 7.

Ronaldo didn’t disappoint: He showed his promise early on in the 2004 FA Cup final, scoring the team’s first three goals and helping them capture the championship. In 2007, Ronaldo signed a five-year, £31 million contract. A year later, Ronaldo again justified his high salary when he put together one of the club’s finest seasons in history, setting a franchise record for goals scored (42) and earning himself the FIFA World Player of the Year honor for 2008. In all, Ronaldo helped steer Manchester United to three Premier League titles.
